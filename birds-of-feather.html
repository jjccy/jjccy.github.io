Project overview

Project Description

"Birds of a Feather" is a one month team VR project with Unity from class IAT 445: Immersive Environment. The goal of this project is to raise awareness of protecting our environment. The purpose is to let players get a chance to see how much garbage the animal wilderness can consume unconsciously. The emotion players experience throughout the game could be helplessness and shock, concern or disgust. Our project ties in this semester's course design challenge of environmental issues, we as human are living together with other species on this planet, protecting the environment is akin to protecting ourselves.

Process

Motivation & Inspiration

Each semester the course will provide a different topic for the VR project. For that semester, the topic was about the environment, and our team wants to focus on the impacts of human pollution on wild animals.

We draw inspiration from Jenny Jinya's short comic "Seabirds and Plastics from u", which uses personification and exaggeration to illustrate how seabirds are affected by human pollution. Use this short comic as inspiration, we quickly confirmed one key concept: birds can't tell the difference between food and garbage. Base on this concept the rough storyline and the general UX finish in no time, myself and one other team member constantly fixing the storyline and event to better tell the story.

Storyline

The story begins with the introduction of the character’s role. Beginning as a human, you are transported into the essence of an egg. After hatching, you become a baby bird. Each day you are brought food by your care-providing mother-bird. Your goal is to feed and survive the harsh conditions of the natural environment. You will be eating the food collected by your mother, but some of it will be garbage and you counldn't tell. The player will be able to explore the nest while mother soars through the sky and will pick up food for the young bird. Both birds need to eat to survive, both the baby bird and big bird will become sick when they consume too much garbage-food.

Mechanism

Once the general storyline is confirmed, we separate the workload and work on our own parts. While my teammates are working on most of the models and animations, I was responsible for most of almost all the mechanisms and most of the codes. The very first thing I was working are the two most important mechanisms: break free from the egg and auto spawn food.

The first one I was working on breaks free from the egg with the virtual beak attaches at the player's mouth position. Detect if the beak hits the egg is the easy part, collision detection will do the work, the hard part is where egg cracks open after some hits.

The first thing to do is to model the egg that is about to hatch. Once got that, the next thing is to make this egg model breaks into small pieces, which took most of the time on this mechanism. The basic idea is simple: when beak hits the egg more than a certain number, replace the original egg with the egg has cracks, the egg breaks. The Challenge thing is how to let the crack egg "explode". My solution is to make all eggshells collider a bit bigger than it looks. Therefore, when the egg is replaced with the cracked egg, all eggshells collide into each other, which gets pushed away by the physics engine, create the explosion effect. Once some sound effects and artificial shaking is added, the whole mechanism is done.

The mother bird's only mission in this game is to feed her babies, therefore, it needs to spawn foods when needed. I was having challenges when creating this mechanism, I got the food that needs to spawn, but how to spawn them? I wasn't sure how to achieve this problem until I found a tutorial about how to create a functional gun in Unity, which teaches how to create a duplicate for designed objects at a designed location. To prevent having too much copy of food will slow the running speed, I also make the copy food self destroy after some time period.

Cut out mechanisms

Not all mechanisms could stay until the end, there are a lot of mechanisms were change or even delete at the end. The video on the side is a pitch video made in the middle of the project, it got most of the model and the environment at the final version but still, lack of detail.

You could tell from the video in our original design, player could visit outside of the nest. However, this is soon removed after this video because it gives the player too much freedom instead of experience the story. Original the game also has day and night, the entire story happens across couple days in-game time, but we realize that it will cause the story become too lengthy, players will get bored before the end of the story.

How Birds See The World
Neringa Utaraitė

While players couldn't identify the difference between foods and garbage also didn't realize what they were eating might be garbage, we thought a lot of ways to hint what they were eating might be garbage. These include but not limited to:

  • apply different physics on food and garbage
  • human action and conversation at the side
  • slowly reveal the blur filter on food and garbage as time pass

There are many more fun ideas like this, but one of the most interesting ideas was inspired by how birds and humans see colour differently. We were thinking to change all colour in the game world to a different colour palate to "mimic" how birds see the world. In that case, players won't tell the difference between cigarettes and worm, other than its movement.

This idea finally got redesigned because it is too aline like, which will break the immersive. However, it helps us to re-think our approach and lead us to the current way to create models for food and garbage.
